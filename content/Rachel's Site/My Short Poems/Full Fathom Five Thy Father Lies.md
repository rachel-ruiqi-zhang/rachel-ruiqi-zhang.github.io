This is a translational attempt of the famous excerpt from Shakespeare's *The Tempest*, asked by a close friend in college back in 2021：

<pre style="white-space: pre-wrap; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, 'Nimbus Roman No9 L', 'KaiTi', 'STKaiti', 'SimSun', serif; font-size: 1.15rem; line-height: 1.9; background: none; border: none; padding: 0; margin: 1em 0;">
Full Fathom five thy father lies;
整五英寻，汝父安处
Of his bones are coral made;
珊瑚制身骨
Those are pearls that were his eyes:
珍珠为眼珠
Nothing of him that doth fade,
他未曾黯淡
But doth suffer a sea-change
而沧海桑田
Into something rich and strange.
他富丽奇灿
Sea-nymphs hourly ring his knell:
海妖屡现，丧钟鸣响
Hark! now I hear them-Ding-dong, bell.
听！今吾闻之--钟声叮当
